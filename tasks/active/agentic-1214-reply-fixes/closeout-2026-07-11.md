## Goal

Resolve every finding from the comparison against `main`, correct the unsafe

## What shipped

- Native reply retry now uses a persisted identity capsule, not
  `outgoing message.id`. `reply_draft_resolver_scripts.py` takes a complete
  bounded Drafts snapshot, requires the saved count to increase by exactly one,
  and accepts only one new draft whose RFC `Message-ID` and `In-Reply-To` link
  it to the source. `reply_identity.py` rejects malformed capsules.
- Exact verification and delete-and-retype revalidate the same persisted RFC
  headers at the numeric Drafts ID. Missing, capped, delayed, ambiguous, or
  drifted identity disables automatic cleanup and leaves bounded fallback
  diagnostic only.
- `verify_draft(expected_body_contains=...)` now treats only Apple Mail and
  Outlook attribution structures as quote boundaries. A bare authored
  `wrote:` phrase checks against the complete preview.
- The cross-cutting suite directly compiles the generated native reply script.
  Regression coverage exercises capsules, drift, ambiguity, fallback refusal,
  and delete protection.
- v3.11.2 synchronizes the six public version surfaces, README, maintainer
  guide, packaged workflow skills, changelog, task pointers, and the review
  resolution record.

## Decision record

The earlier recommendation to expose `id of replyMessage` was rejected after a
live Exchange check proved it is a transient outgoing-message namespace, not
the persisted Drafts ID. The release therefore prefers a bounded, provable
identity over an always-available but unsafe numeric handle.
